Residential Market (2026)¶

  • Price Growth: Forecasts predict slow national price increases (around 1-2%), with real (inflation-adjusted) prices potentially declining, making homes more affordable relative to income.
  • Mortgage Rates: Rates are expected to decrease, helping offset price gains and improving monthly affordability.
  • Inventory: Supply is gradually improving but remains below pre-pandemic levels, especially in the Northeast and Midwest.
  • Sales: Existing home sales are predicted to rise as affordability improves and more homeowners list properties.
  • Regional Differences: Markets in the South and West are more balanced due to increased construction, while the Northeast and Midwest still lag in inventory.

  • others are choosing to walk away and come back at a later date because they have the flexibility to wait.
  • “Using NAR month-supply data, the housing market is the most balanced it’s been in almost a decade.

# Compute ROI
fixup = 10000
salePrice = (askPrice * 0.90) - fixup

mktRate = 0.08
roiYears = 10

reRateExp = 0.20 # Rental PropTax/Maintainance Expense


def apprValue(PV, R, years):
    p = PV
    for i in range(years):
        p = p * (1.0 + R)

    return int(p)

def fvDF(salePrice, nm, **kwargs):

    rent = kwargs.get('rent', 2600)

    mktFV = apprValue(salePrice, mktRate, roiYears)
    reInc = int(rent*12*roiYears*0.8)
    reFVProp = apprValue(salePrice, reRate, roiYears)
    reFV = reFVProp + reInc
    return pd.DataFrame([dict(SalePrice = int(salePrice), 
                        FV_mkt_8Pct = mktFV, 
                        FV_RE = reFV,
                        FV_mkt_10Pct = apprValue(salePrice, 0.1, roiYears),
                        FV_RE_Prop = reFVProp,
                        FV_RE_Income = reInc,
                        PerYr_mkt = int(mktFV/roiYears), 
                        PerYr_RE = int(reFV/roiYears)
                       )], index=[nm])
roiDF = pd.concat([fvDF(240000,'sale240K'),
           fvDF(265000, 'Sale265K'),
           fvDF(277000, 'Sale277K')
          ]).transpose()
