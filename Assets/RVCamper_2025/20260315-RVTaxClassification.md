# IRS Guide: Form 1065 - RV purchased for Future Service

When an LLC purchases an RV in December to renovate and rent out in a future year, 
- RV is generally not depreciated in the year of purchase.
- treated as an asset under construction,
- expenses are capitalized until the asset is "placed in service" (ready for its intended use as a rental). 

Here is how to report this on your Form 1065:

## 1. Balance Sheet Reporting (Schedule L)

- Because the RV is not yet producing income and is being renovated
- it should be recorded on the Balance Sheet (Schedule L) as an asset. 
- Ledger Recording 
    - Asset Categorization: 
        - "Other Assets"
        - "Tools, Machinery, Equipment"
        - depending on your chart of accounts.
    - Construction in Progress:
        - The initial purchase price
        - all renovation costs (materials, repairs, upgrades) should be added together
        - reported as "Construction in Progress"
        - capitalized into the cost basis of the asset. 

## 2. Expenses and Depreciation
No Depreciation Yet: You cannot begin depreciation until the RV is placed in service (i.e., available for rent).
Capitalize Expenses: Expenses incurred in December to get the RV ready for rent (fixing it up) are not deductible immediately. They must be added to the cost basis of the RV.
Start-up Costs: Costs incurred before the rental business actively begins, such as insurance, advertising, or registration, might be considered startup costs or deferred until the business opens. 

## 3. When the RV is "Placed in Service" (Future Year)
Once the RV is finished and listed for rent:
Depreciation: You will start depreciating the total capitalized cost (purchase price + improvements) using Form 4562. RVs are typically depreciated over a 5-year period.
Reporting Income: The rental income and expenses will be reported, often on Schedule K (as ordinary business income) or Form 8825, depending on how your tax professional classifies the rental type. 

## 4. Important Considerations
Personal Use: If you, the partners, or friends use the RV personally at any point, you must keep strict records to allocate the business use vs. personal use percentage, as only the business portion is deductible.
December Purchase: Even though it was bought in December, you likely won't take a deduction until the next tax year when it is operational.
