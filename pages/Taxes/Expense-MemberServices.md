# Expensing Member Services/Income

By default, a multi-member LLC 
- is treated as a `partnership` for tax purposes. 

## How members get paid: 
- Multi-member LLC owners can receive income in two main ways:

### Guaranteed Payments
  - These are payments made to members for services rendered, 
  - Treated similarly to a salary. 
  - The LLC can deduct guaranteed payments as a business expense
  - Member reports them as self-employment income.

### Profit Distributions
  - each member's share of the company's profits 
  - based on their ownership percentage. 
  - Distributions are not a deductible expense for the LLC.

## Reporting LLC Labor expense: 
  - LLC files (**Form 1065**) 
  - will report the guaranteed payments as a deductible expense, 
  - reduces the business's taxable income.

## Reporting Member Taxes 
  - Members receive a Schedule K-1 
  - detailing their share of profits and any guaranteed payments. 
  - Members pay income tax and self-employment tax on their total compensation.
