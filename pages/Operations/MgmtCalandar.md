### 2026.03.15 :: Form 1065, `U.S. Return of Partnership Income`
    - Partnerships file an information return to report their income, gains, losses, deductions, credits, etc.
    - A partnership does not pay tax on its income but "passes through" any profits or losses to its partners.
    - Partners must include partnership items on their tax or information returns.
    - questions; due dates shown, you can call us at the phone number or write to us at the address shown at the top of this notice.
    - If you need help in determining your annual accounting period (tax year), see Publication 538, Accounting Periods and Methods.
    - the income from the LLC (multi-member) will be taxable on your return
    - treated as partnerships
    - Partnership income is “passed-through” to the partners returns by their proportionate ownership percentage.
    - the partners all pay tax on this income
    - even if the partnership doesn’t actually distribute any of the income to the partners.
    - a member's income is based on their share of the net profits (or losses), not the gross rental income
    - **FORM 1065 - LLC Files an Information Return** :
          - `Form 1065`, U.S. Return of Partnership Income.
          - The multi-member LLC itself does not pay federal income tax.
          - It files an `informational tax return` with the IRS
          - reports the total gross income and deductible expenses of the business.
    - **Net Profit Calculation**:
          The net profit = gross rental income - BUSINESS_EXPENSES
          - all eligible business expenses (such as mortgage interest, property taxes, maintenance costs, and depreciation)
          - details their individual share of the net profits (or losses), deductions, and credits.
